01

Payments as code through a single ledger.

Payment intent, settlement, and compliance run on separate systems today — that's why workflows break. Atrya is a native PSP that collapses them into one regulated ledger where intent is settlement. No reconciliation. No off-rail finality. No batch windows.

02

The end of correspondent banking.

Cross-border payments move through five intermediaries, three days, two reconciliation systems. Atrya replaces them with one ledger — EMTs connecting SEPA, PIX, CLABE, and beyond. FX locked upfront. Global payouts, settled in minutes.

03

Money for autonomous systems.

AI agents can research, decide, and act — but they can't pay. Bank rails need human accounts and credentials no agent should hold. Atrya gives agents their own compliant wallet on programmable EMT rails. The agent becomes the client. One key, no humans in the loop.

04

Regulation as architecture.

Payment regulation has always been fragmented — harmonized only by slow treaties. EMTs change that: the same stablecoin is regulated e-money under MiCAR in Europe and the GENIUS Act in the US. Decentralization is doing what diplomacy couldn't. Atrya operates the first regulated on-chain FX facility in Europe — compliance isn't a constraint, it's what makes the product possible.

Vision

The core problem no one solved

B2B payment workflows break because payment logic and settlement live in separate systems. Formats, compliance rules, and FX data are incompatible across rails — creating higher fees, slower settlement, and heavy operations.

Atrya is building a native PSP architecture where execution, settlement, and compliance happen in one transaction layer. EMTs replace legacy settlements and turn payments into programmable transactions.

For the first time, logic and finality are unified — enabling automated B2B workflows, agentic commerce, and cross-border flows that simply work.
