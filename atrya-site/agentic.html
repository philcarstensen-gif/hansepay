The gap

Legacy rails were built
for humans.

Every existing payment rail was designed around a human account holder with a government-issued identity. AI agents break every assumption these systems were built on.

No financial identity
Agents can't open bank accounts, hold funds, or settle obligations without a human account holder as intermediary. Every payment introduces a bottleneck and a single point of failure.
Approval loops kill autonomy
Existing rails require human sign-off at every significant transaction. The autonomous agent becomes a proposal generator — not an executor. Latency and friction accumulate at every step.
Workarounds, not infrastructure
Virtual cards and API keys bolted onto legacy banking weren't designed for machine actors. They introduce hard limits, compliance blind spots, and architectures that break as complexity scales.
The architecture

Native primitives for
the machine economy.

Atrya doesn't adapt banking for agents. It builds from first principles — a financial layer designed around how autonomous systems actually operate.

01
Agent Wallets
Issue a regulated EMT wallet to any agent, task, or workflow in milliseconds. Each wallet carries a ledger identity — not just an API key. Spend limits, allowlists, and full audit trails enforced at the protocol level.
02
Programmable Settlement
Payment logic lives in code, not a workflow tool. Define triggers, multi-party splits, conditional releases, and escrow that execute at ledger level — deterministic, composable, real-time.
03
Compliance as Architecture
Regulatory obligations embedded at the infrastructure layer, not checked after the fact. Agents operate within defined parameters without human approval per transaction. The ledger is the compliance record.

Use cases

Built for autonomous systems at every scale.

Autonomous Procurement
Agents source, evaluate, and pay for services within predefined budget parameters — no purchase order, no approval workflow, no delay.
Pay-per-inference Billing
Micro-settlements between AI services metered to the individual request. From fractions of a cent to enterprise volumes — settled at the transaction layer.
Multi-Agent Coordination
Shared escrow, programmatic cost splits, and peer-to-peer settlement across distributed agent pipelines. Funds move when conditions are met — not when a human approves.
Cross-Border Treasury
Agents manage FX exposure and execute cross-border movements at machine speed. Deterministic rates, instant settlement — no T+2, no correspondent banking lag.
Compliance-Gated Releases
Define compliance rules as code. Payments clear only when conditions are satisfied — counterparty checks, spend limits, time windows, jurisdiction rules enforced at the ledger.
Audit by Default
Every wallet, every transaction, every agent action recorded immutably at ledger level. Full traceability without a separate logging layer — the ledger is the audit trail.
